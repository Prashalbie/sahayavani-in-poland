Legal & Visa

Last updated: June 2025 · Verified against gov.pl official sources

⚠️ Immigration rules in Poland change frequently. Always cross-check with the official gov.pl portal before submitting any application.

What documents do you need first?

Before applying for any permit, gather these essentials. Missing even one document is the most common reason applications get rejected at the counter.

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond your intended stay)
  • Recent passport-size photographs (35mm x 45mm, white background)
  • Proof of accommodation in Poland (rental agreement or hotel booking)
  • Employment contract or university enrolment letter
  • Health insurance — NFZ card or private policy covering all of Poland
  • Proof of sufficient funds (bank statements, last 3 months)
💡 Community tip: Get all documents translated into Polish by a sworn translator (tłumacz przysięgły). Unofficial translations are rejected. Cost is typically 50–80 PLN per page.

Types of residence permits

Most Indians in Poland fall into one of these three categories:

01

Temporary Residence Permit (Karta Pobytu Czasowego)

For stays up to 3 years. Most common for employees and students. Can be renewed. Processing time: 1–6 months depending on the voivodeship (region).

02

Permanent Residence Permit (Karta Stałego Pobytu)

Requires 5 continuous years of legal stay in Poland, or 3 years if married to a Polish citizen. Gives the right to work without a separate work permit.

03

EU Long-term Resident Permit

After 5 years of legal stay. Allows you to move and work in other EU countries. Requires proof of stable income and integration (basic Polish language).

Step-by-step: Applying for Karta Pobytu

1

Book an appointment (wizyta)

Go to your regional Urząd Wojewódzki (voivodeship office) website and book online. Do NOT show up without an appointment — you will be turned away. Appointments fill up fast; book 2–3 months in advance.

2

Submit the application form

Download form "Wniosek o udzielenie zezwolenia na pobyt czasowy" from gov.pl. Fill it in Polish. Bring 2 copies to your appointment.

3

Pay the stamp duty (opłata skarbowa)

340 PLN for temporary permit. Pay at the office cashier or via bank transfer to the city council account before your appointment. Bring the receipt.

4

Get your fingerprints taken

Done at the office during your appointment. Children under 6 are exempt. You cannot do this step online.

5

Collect your Karta Pobytu

After approval (1–6 months), you'll receive a letter. Return to the office to collect your physical card. Bring your passport and the collection notice.

🕐 While you wait: If your visa expires during processing, the "pieczątka" (stamp) in your passport proves your application is under review and you are legally in Poland. Carry it everywhere.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Wrong address registered: Your Karta Pobytu address must match your actual registered address (meldunek). Update meldunek first if you moved.
  • Expired employer confirmation: The employer's letter must be dated within 3 months of your application.
  • Missing apostille: Documents from India (birth certificate, marriage certificate) need an apostille from the Indian Ministry of External Affairs before being accepted in Poland.
  • Not checking status online: Use the reference number from your submission receipt to track your case at the voivodeship office website.
